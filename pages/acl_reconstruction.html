ACL Reconstruction

The ACL is the anterior cruciate ligament - a major ligament within the knee. It connects the femur (thighbone) to the tibia (shinbone), and provides stability to the knee, preventing abnormal movement between the two bones. Specifically, it provides rotational stability to the knee, allowing movements such as pivoting or sudden changes in direction without the knee giving way. In ACL reconstruction, the torn ligament is removed and replaced with a piece of tendon from another part of your knee or from a donor.

ACL Injury

The most common mechanicsm of injury occurs when attempting a sudden pivot or change in direction, typically when playing a sport. The injury can also occur from landing from a jump incorectly, or receiving a direct blow to the knee. Not uncommonly, many people hear or feel a "pop" in the knee when an ACL injury occurs.

Symptoms

Signs and symptoms of an ACL injury usually include:
  • A loud "pop" or a "popping" sensation in the knee
  • Severe pain and inability to continue activity
  • Rapid swelling
  • Loss of range of motion
  • Feeling of instability when weight bearing
